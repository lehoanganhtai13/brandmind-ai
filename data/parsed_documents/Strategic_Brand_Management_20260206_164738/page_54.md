This data presents a comparative analysis of brand strength growth between 2007 and 2010, ranking ten distinct brands by their percentage increase. Facebook exhibited the most significant growth at 195%, followed by Skype with 79% and YouTube with 78%. Other brands showing substantial growth include Netflix (72%), Samsung (66%), and Apple (51%). Brands with more moderate growth rates were iTunes (50%), Amazon.com (44%), Reese’s (42%), and National Guard (35%). This data presents a comparative analysis of various sources influencing consumer purchasing decisions, ranked by the percentage of respondents who cite them. Friends and peers emerge as the most influential source at 81%, followed closely by fashion magazines at 68% and advertisements at 58%. Company websites and consumer reviews hold moderate influence, at 44% and 36% respectively. Celebrities and parents/adults exert less influence, with 33% and 25% respectively. Bloggers represent the least influential source among those surveyed, at 14%. The structure is a simple ranking, highlighting the relative importance of different information channels in shaping consumer choices.
