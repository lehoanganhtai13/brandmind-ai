# FIGURE 20.1  
## Sustainable Marketing

This table outlines the evolution of marketing concepts based on the changing needs of consumers and businesses. Initially, businesses focused on the "Marketing concept" to meet current consumer demands. Looking towards the future, businesses adopted a "Strategic planning concept" to anticipate and address evolving consumer needs. Concurrently, the needs of consumers themselves shifted, leading to the development of the "Societal marketing concept," which considers broader societal welfare alongside consumer satisfaction. The ultimate future state, highlighted in red, represents the "Sustainable marketing concept," indicating a comprehensive approach that integrates business strategies, future consumer needs, and long-term environmental and social sustainability. This demonstrates a progression from a product-centric to a strategically planned, societally aware, and ultimately sustainable marketing paradigm.

> The marketing concept means meeting the current needs of both customers and the company. But that can sometimes mean compromising the future of both.

> Sustainable marketing means meeting current needs in a way that preserves the rights and options of future generations of consumers and businesses.
